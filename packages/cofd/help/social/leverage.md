social leverage  -- Soft vs hard leverage rules.

Leverage is information or pressure used to bypass a door directly,
or to shift the subject's impression.

Soft leverage (+social/soft <kind>=<text>):
  aspiration   Removes a door. Tying the maneuver to one of the
               subject's Aspirations gives them a reason to comply.
  vice         Bumps impression up one step. Appealing to the
               subject's Vice softens their stance.
  gift         Bumps impression up one step. A meaningful gift,
               favour, or gesture warms them to you.

Hard leverage (+social/hard [severe] <text>):
  Threat, blackmail, or coercion. Removes one door (two if 'severe').
  Worsens impression by one step. May trigger your character's
  Breaking Point (see help integrity) if the act is severe enough.

Limits:
  Each soft-leverage kind may be used once per maneuver. Hard
  leverage may be used repeatedly but the impression cost compounds.

See also: social, social doors, social impression, integrity
