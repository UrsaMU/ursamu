+tilt scope  -- Personal vs Environmental Tilts, scene lifecycle.

Personal Tilts:
  Apply to one character. Caused by attacks (specified targets, wound
  thresholds) or supernatural powers. The catalog lists the Causing
  vector and Ending condition for each one. Examples:
    Arm Wrack, Beaten Down, Blinded, Deafened, Drugged, Immobilized,
    Insane, Insensate, Knocked Down, Leg Wrack, Poisoned, Sick,
    Stunned.

Environmental Tilts:
  Affect a whole scene -- weather, terrain, supernatural disasters.
  Track them by adding the same Tilt to every affected character.
  Examples:
    Blizzard, Earthquake, Extreme Cold, Extreme Heat, Flooded,
    Heavy Rain, Heavy Winds, Ice.

Beats:
  Resolving or removing a Tilt awards no Beats (CoFD 2e core p.282).
  Earning Beats from danger or misfortune happens through Aspirations,
  Breaking Points, and Condition resolutions instead.

End-of-scene:
  At scene end, use +tilt/clear to sweep every active Tilt off the
  sheet. Personal Tilts with wound-tracked endings (Arm Wrack, Leg
  Wrack, Blinded, Deafened) should be removed individually as the
  underlying damage heals, not by /clear.

Cross-reference:
  Some Conditions and Tilts share names (Blind vs Blinded, Insane vs
  Madness). The Conditions catalog covers the long-term persistent
  versions; the Tilts catalog covers the in-combat versions.

See also: tilt, condition, health
